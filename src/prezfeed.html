Aggregate social feed concept, with content involving the presidential elections.

Visual Design and UX

Rochester, NY Winter 2015

    PrezFeed is an online app that allows users to follow their choice of presidential candidate through the use of a composite news feed populated by sources like Facebook, Twitter, CNN, Youtube, and many more. Users can keep up with their candidate’s poll results, social relevance, and other live statistics all in real time. Sources can be selected and filtered based on preference, with new sources suggested on occasion.


    PrezFeed can help inform voters about their candidate in a structured and streamlined interface, and can fix the problem of misinformation through arbitrary media exposure. This is an unexplored need with profitable options, either through advertisements on a free application or using a paid model.

    Research was conducted in order to better meet the needs and wants of the users, and to target our application to focus on key areas that are wanted. In addition, it also enables our application to match the quality of current apps on both separate app stores. Research included user surveys and analysis of similar mobile applications. A functional prototype was used in heuristics and usability studies. Task scenarios guided our initial prototype and helped us understand the user’s flow in the application. Tasks that required the user to go through multiple views were more difficult- however after doing navigation between different pages the user was able to traverse the application with ease, showing good levels of recall in the system (but again, ultimately lower levels of discovery).
